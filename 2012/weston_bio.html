John H. Weston, Member of the Board and Chief Marketing and Development Officer, Marshall & Stevens

Mr. Weston joined Marshall & Stevens in 2009 and is responsible for the expansion and execution of marketing and corporate development activities including strategic planning, ecommerce, CRM, strategic alliances and partnerships. Mr. Weston has previously served in corporate development, marketing, communications and strategic planning roles for FedEx, Mars, Inc. and several professional services companies. Mr. Weston is also a member of the Board of Directors of Marshall & Stevens working out of the corporate headquarters in Los Angeles.

Prior to his advisement role at Marshall & Stevens, Mr. Weston served as Vice President Business Development for private equity backed CaseStack, a technology based supply chain services company. During this time he managed the marketing, sales, business development, pricing and client development functions. Private equity partners included Clarity Partners, Blumberg Capital, Kline Hawkes, and Garage Technologies. Previously Mr. Weston served as Vice President Marketing, Sales, Pricing and Business Development for FedEx Freight, a $4 billion FedEx operating company. As an officer for FedEx Freight, he managed over 800 sales, marketing and pricing professionals. Mr. Weston served on FedEx Corporation operating company committee assignments and chaired the FedEx Freight charitable and community development program. In addition, Mr. Weston served in management and marketing strategy roles as Executive Director for Catalina Marketing and Vice President of Business Development for Montague & Associates, a management strategy consulting firm. Key clients served by Mr. Weston include Nestle, Coca Cola, ConAgra, Frito Lay, and the Transportation Corridor Agency. From 1983 through 1995 Mr. Weston held numerous strategic planning, marketing, sales and business development positions for consumer product industry leader Mars, Inc.

Mr. Weston received his BA in political science and business from Brigham Young University, his MPA in strategy and policy from the Marriott School of Management, Brigham Young University and the Executive Program in Management from Anderson School of Management, UCLA. Professional Affiliations include serving on the board of directors for the Paralysis Project of America and the advisory board of the Brigham Young University Marriott School of Management Marketing.
